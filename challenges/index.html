Challenges

When building your sustainable home, you might run into challenges along the way, but we're here to help you get through them.

Availability & Environmental

When you are considering on-site generation, you might face several challenges along the way. To overcome these you can look into the availability
of renewable resources in your area, consider the prices of different renewable technologies, examine the benefits of on-site power, and find available sources for financing.

When building a sustainable home, zoning can be a problem. Especially with wind energy, since it takes up so much space it could ruin habitats when installing them. To fix this problem, you can simply downsize to a smaller wind farm, or use a different type of renewable energy.

Pricing & Laws

Using solar energy presents several problems, one of which are property deeds that restrict the use of solar energy, problems like these are not easy to solve, but it can be done, In almost every state in America, homeowners can negotiate for their rights to solar energy use.

Building a sustainable home can be a financial struggle with heavily competitive markets and the long term investments. To overcome this, try looking into where you want to build your home, what type of energy source you want to use, and what type of payment plan would be best.

Our Mission

By providing information about solar, wind energy, geothermal, and earthship bioshelters, homeowners are satisfied with their energy choices.
